\# Founding Session 001 (Continued)



\*\*Date:\*\* 2026-06-30



The initial design discussions significantly refined the intellectual architecture of AIM-SEA. The project evolved from an assessment framework into a broader research effort focused on the development of intellectually defensible educational assessment frameworks.



Major architectural decisions:



\- Defensibility was adopted as the project's foundational axiom.

\- Rigor was redefined as the primary objective achieved through defensible scholarship rather than as a foundational axiom.

\- Epistemology was identified as the foundational architectural layer of the project.

\- The project architecture was organized into four layers:

&#x20; 1. Epistemology

&#x20; 2. Methodology

&#x20; 3. Framework Specifications

&#x20; 4. Assessment Content

\- The framework formally distinguished between specifications, hypotheses, research questions, and unknowns.

\- The development process was revised to emphasize scholarly review, explicit assumptions, documented reasoning, and continuous revision.

\- Athena's role expanded from curriculum architect to include editorial responsibility for intellectual defensibility and architectural consistency.



Outcome:



The project concluded that AIM-SEA is not merely an assessment framework. It is a methodology for constructing intellectually defensible educational assessment frameworks, with AI-enabled mobile software engineering serving as its initial application domain.
